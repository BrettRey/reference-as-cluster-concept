# Structured Abstract Draft

## Problem
Coelho Mollo & Millière (2026) frame the Vector Grounding Problem as explaining how vector systems manipulated numerically can acquire extra-linguistic reference without interpreter dependence.

## Gap
Their taxonomy productively distinguishes grounding notions, but what the debate calls "referential grounding" bundles four theoretically distinct relations from different philosophical traditions. The target paper acknowledges this implicitly (fn 11, p. 8, p. 9) but doesn't decompose it, inviting a binary grounded/ungrounded framing that obscures graded and mechanism-heterogeneous stabilization.

## Thesis
What the grounding debate calls "referential grounding" is a cluster concept: it bundles teleosemantic selection, causal-informational coupling, communicative calibration, and inferential-role structure. These strands come from different research programmes, co-occur in paradigmatic grounders, but can dissociate. Recognizing this dissolves the binary impasse and supports a projectible groundedness profile.

## Method
Conceptual reconstruction of the target argument, then comparative synthesis across teleosemantics, communicative calibration, and grounding debate interlocutors.

## Contribution
The paper preserves the normative demand for correctness conditions while replacing solved/unsolved verdicts with a grounding profile framework.

## Payoff
This reframing reduces metaphysical overpressure and converts the core dispute into testable questions about which training/deployment mechanisms stabilize world-directed success conditions.
