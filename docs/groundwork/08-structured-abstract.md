# Structured Abstract Draft

## Problem
Coelho Mollo & Millière (2026) frame the Vector Grounding Problem as explaining how vector systems manipulated numerically can acquire extra-linguistic reference without interpreter dependence.

## Gap
Their taxonomy productively distinguishes grounding notions, but the discussion invites a binary grounded/un-grounded framing that obscures graded and mechanism-heterogeneous stabilization profiles.

## Thesis
Referential grounding should be modeled as a cluster property with degree structure, where teleosemantic selection history is one central stabilizer among others.

## Method
Conceptual reconstruction of the target argument, then comparative synthesis across teleosemantics, communicative calibration, and grounding debate interlocutors.

## Contribution
The paper preserves the normative demand for correctness conditions while replacing solved/unsolved verdicts with a grounding profile framework.

## Payoff
This reframing reduces metaphysical overpressure and converts the core dispute into testable questions about which training/deployment mechanisms stabilize world-directed success conditions.
