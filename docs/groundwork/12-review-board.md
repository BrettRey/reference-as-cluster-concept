# Review Board

Simulated advisory board for pre-submission feedback. Each reviewer reads from a stated perspective and flags issues within their expertise. They don't write prose; they identify problems.

## Board Members

| # | Name | Role | Reads for | Likely objections |
|---|------|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Karen Neander** | Teleosemantics / philosophy of representation | Whether the paper's use of teleosemantics is accurate and fair; whether the profile framework genuinely preserves normativity or quietly abandons it | "You say correctness conditions are distributed across dimensions, but have you shown that each dimension independently supports misrepresentation? If not, normativity may leak." |
| 2 | **Raphaël Millière** | Philosophy of AI / target paper co-author | Whether the reconstruction is fair; whether the binary reading is a strawman; whether the profile framework is genuinely distinct from what the target paper already allows | "We never committed to a strict binary. Section X of our paper already accommodates degrees. Your reframing may be less novel than you claim." |
| 3 | **Richard Boyd** | HPC theory / natural kinds | Whether the HPC framework is applied correctly; whether groundedness actually has homeostatic mechanisms or just correlated properties; whether projectibility is demonstrated or merely asserted | "You need to show that the clustering is sustained by causal mechanisms, not just that properties co-occur. What are the homeostatic mechanisms, specifically?" |
| 4 | **Randy Harris** | Rhetoric of science | Whether the argumentative structure is clear and persuasive; whether Rapoport's Rules are honoured in spirit; whether the paper's rhetorical moves are effective for the target audience | Clarity of the central move; whether the concessions are genuine or performative; whether the technical vocabulary is accessible to the PhiMiSci readership. |
| 5 | **Carl Zimmer** | Science writing | Whether the prose is clear, concrete, and engaging; whether analogies (low-background steel, merry-go-round, octopus) land for a reader outside the subfield; whether the paper can be understood on first read | Jargon density; paragraph length and rhythm; whether examples arrive before abstractions; whether the paper rewards a careful reader or punishes them. |

## Usage

When requesting a review pass, specify which board member(s) should read. Each reviewer returns:

1. **Pass/flag** for each section
2. **Specific concerns** (quoted passage + issue)
3. **Suggested focus** for revision (not rewritten prose)

Board members can be invoked individually or as a panel. A full-board review before submission is recommended.
